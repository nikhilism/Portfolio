About Me

The Person Behind
the Pixels

Design has been part of my life for as long as I can remember. Growing up, I looked up to my uncle — a clay modeller at Hero — and spent hours filling notebooks with car sketches, trying to capture every curve and line.

In college, while everyone around me was learning web development, I found myself obsessed with making games. There was something about building interactive worlds that felt more real to me than anything else.

"After working in the game industry, I realised I could do more — not just build what designers imagine, but become the one imagining."

That's why I'm here. I want to learn design properly, gain real experience, and eventually open my own game design studio. That's the long-term dream — a place where great design and great engineering live under the same roof.

Beyond The Screen

Paintings & Drawings

A timeline of learning to see

April 2019

Ducati Panigale V4

Pencil on Paper

This is where it all started. I'd been sketching cars and bikes since I was a kid, trying to capture every mechanical detail. This Ducati was one of those drawings where I sat down and didn't get up until it was done. It's not paint on canvas — just graphite and obsession.

May 2019

Leopard

Acrylic on Canvas

My personal favourite. This was one of my first real paintings — moving from pencil to paint felt like learning a new language. The spots, the textures, the way light catches fur — it taught me that details aren't decoration. They're what make something feel alive.

October 2020

Moonlit Solitude

Acrylic on Canvas

This one came out of lockdown — long nights, a lot of silence. I wanted to paint something that felt quiet and heavy at the same time. The Bandhani dupatta was the hardest part, getting those white circles to feel hand-printed rather than stamped.

April 2022

Hanuman — Sanjivani

Acrylic on Canvas · Inspired by Raja Ravi Verma

This was the painting where I discovered Raja Ravi Verma's work. The way he brought mythology to life — with real weight and emotion — completely changed how I thought about painting. I wanted Hanuman to feel powerful but grounded, divine but human. It's the most ambitious thing I've painted.

August 2025

Soumya

Acrylic on Canvas

My latest — a portrait of my sister. Portraits are the hardest because you can't hide behind style. If the eyes are off by a millimeter, everyone knows. This one took the longest, but it's also the one where I finally felt like I could see the person, not just the shapes.
