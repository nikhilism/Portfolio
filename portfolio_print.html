Project 01 — Flagship
XR Indian Road
Simulator
Navigating Beautiful Chaos

Indian roads are a sensory overload — a design problem no one has solved properly. What if you could experience that beautiful chaos in VR, feeling every honk, every swerve, every near-miss through your hands on virtual handlebars?

This project reimagines Indian road navigation as an immersive XR experience — where your body becomes the controller and the chaos becomes the design.

The Interaction

VR controllers become handlebars. Wrist rotation maps to throttle. Physical intuition meets digital world — making the player's body the controller.

🐄

The World

Cows, autos, aunties — not as decoration, but as unpredictable agents that force the player to read the environment.

🧠

The Challenge

Motion sickness is the biggest VR design constraint. Every speed curve had to be tuned to keep immersion without nausea.

What I'd Do Differently
"If I started over, I'd spend more time on audio design. The sounds of Indian roads — the layered honking, the vendors, the chaos — are just as important as the visuals for immersion."

Reflection

This painting of Hanuman carrying the Sanjivani mountain was inspired by the works of Raja Ravi Verma — the way he brought mythology to life with emotion and realism.

"I wanted to capture that same energy — divine power grounded in human feeling. Paintings like this remind me that the best design makes you feel something before you understand it."

I paint when I'm not at a screen, mostly because it's the only place where I'm forced to slow down and really look. Each painting teaches me about observation, patience, and the gap between what you see and what you actually notice.
